You hear people say "sumo is cheating" "conventional is the only way." ... come on man, where's the truth? ⁣⁣ Short answer: No, Sumo is not cheating. ⁣ Although Sumo deadlifting has 20-25% less ROM, this doesn't make this style of lifting necessarily "easier" if compared to the conventional. ⁣ ⁣ ⁣ Even though there's a reduction in ROM, both lifts tend to have an average of 5-8 seconds to complete maximal load reps (1RMs). ⁣ ⁣ Sumo deadlifts are typically (NOT ALWAYS!) more difficult off the ground but easier at lock out, while Conventional DLs are the opposite.⁣ ⁣⁣ When it comes to these two different styles of pulling, different set ups will maximally activate different muscles. However, when it comes to the main differences in muscle activity, according to EMG studies, there are mainly just two. In fact:⁣ ⁣⁣ Sumo deadlifts will put a greater emphasis on the quads (when pulling the bar off the floor), compared to a Conventional Style DL. ⁣⁣ Conventional deadlifts will put a greater emphasis on the erector spinae (when pulling the bar off the floor), compared to a Sumo Style DL. ⁣⁣ ⁣⁣ Hamstrings/glutes activity results to be about the same. ⁣⁣ ⁣⁣ Personally I think they both are amazing exercises, and that both should be trained.
